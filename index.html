About Me

Me as a person

So...to start this off. I'm Roberto and I've been living in Croatia for like 17-ish years now. Born in the Netherlands however, so I have cultural characteristics of two sides of Europe. Which resulted in a funny combo where I love to crack jokes and have fun, but am I'm also a stickler with scheduling (mostly for myself).

I enjoy a lot of things, starting of with the general IT stuff. I've been fiddling with PCs since I can remember. Always loved working on them and with them. It started with just loving to game and evolved pretty quickly from there. Which relates to another hobby of mine, which is gaming. I enjoy gaming a lot, one of my favourite past-times. Strategy, MMOs, RPGs, party games, you name it. Softly related to that, I'm a big fan of TTRPGs (TableTop RolePlaying Games), the main being D&D. I'm avid storyteller in such games, especially fantasy related.

Easy to say that some of those hobbies really made me diligent in things, creating an eye for details. Especially due to story-writing where you have to be careful with all the details so nothing contradicts or leave room for misunderstanding. I try my best often and will go out of my way to help.

Previous Work

All of it can be found on my Github

Apapp was my final university project to gain my Bachelor's degree. The mobile Android app is essentially a glorified calender-planner. Mainly aimed for people who rent their apartments to tourists here in Croatia during the summer.

Fully written in Kotlin, it had several more unique functionalities, like showing upcoming bookings, hiding those that have past (with filter options to actually show). Simple and intuitive UI, tested with people who aren't as tech-savy. Which was my main aim.

Hero Picker was a small project I did during my university time. Written in C#, we had to make a simple Windows app with several guidelines to follow. As I was pretty big in Overwatch at the time, that is what I based the app on. It's a 'personality' test where the user answers several questions and then a result of which hero would suite their style the best.

Other than a personality quiz, it also kept track of the user's results and showed it as a graph in the user's profile tab.

Calverter was a simple Java app a colleague and I made as a project. Written fully in Java, we made a calculator that was also able to convert measurements when needed. Distance, mass and time were the main converted variables we added to it.

While it was fully written in Java, as the professor was insistent on using Java, I did my part in Kotlin and converted it to Java, which might've not been the best idea, but it worked in the end.
